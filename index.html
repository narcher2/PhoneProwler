INTERVIEW SIMULATOR

  • High Concept: Answer these interview questions correctly to get that dream job you've always wanted!
  • Player Experience Goals: Critical thinking, psychological analysis of answers based on framing of questions.
  • Genre: Text-based simulation
  • Game play: Answering multiple-choice questions in interview form.
  • Prototype goal: Text based navigation of a game, different pathways from different questions.
  • Features: The game will feature facial expressions that the player interprets, as returning a good/bad/neutral answer, and should use these interpretations to modify future answers.
  • Setting/Premise: Takes place in an office, with the intimidating boss of the job of your dreams staring down at you.
  • Story: You've always wanted to be a USER_INPUT_POSITION, and now is finally your opportunity to get it. You step inside the office, ankles quivering as you shake your interviewer's firm handshake and sit down.
  • Target Audience: The unemployed.
  • Play time: Three minutes.
  • Strategies: Figure out what traits the interviewer likes- confidence, humility, intelligence, strength, leadership, etc. and answer your questions in that fashion.

    Phone Prowler

    • High Concept: Role play as a detective. Search through the criminal's confiscated phone to discover his secret... or secrets.
    • Player Experience Goals: Humor, exploration, empathy.
    • Genre: Phone simulation.
    • Game play: Navigating through text messages.
    • Prototype goal: Text based navigation of a phone, different contacts have different messages that can lead you to piece together the man's life.
    • Features: The game will feature realistic text message dialogue that can be analyzed on deeper levels.
    • Setting/Premise: Takes place in the virtual world of a cell phone.
    • Story: Chuck Bronson was just arrested. They can't pin anything on him yet. Your job as an intern, an assistant to the lead detective, is to search through his cell phone message to gain enough evidence to put him away for good.
    • Target Audience: Those who like reading, invading other people's lives, solving mysteries, etc.
    • Play time: Depends on how fast the user can read.
    • Strategies: It's an open world exploration type of thing, there isn't really much strategy besides making sure to explore all corners.
